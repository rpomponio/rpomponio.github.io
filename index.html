Raymond Pomponio, BSc

I am a data analyst at CBICA, a computational imaging lab. In 2018 I began working here after receiving my bachelor's at the University of Pennsylvania.

I primarily work on the statistical analysis of medical images in neuroscience. Recently, we developed a statistically-based harmonization technique for pooling large neuroimaging datasets across the lifespan. The method, called ComBat+GAM, was recently published in Neuroimage and utilizes generalized additive models (GAMs) to estimate robust age trends in structural brain measurements, while simultaneously correcting for machine-related variance between datasets.
